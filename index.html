Selena's Story

I've been fighting since I was 10.

My name is Selena — I'm a senior at the University of Maryland, and I've been living with lupus since I was diagnosed at age 10. For most of my life, I've learned how to manage it, push through it, and keep going.

But lupus eventually caught up with my kidneys. The disease caused serious kidney damage, and on top of that, I went through several months of chemotherapy as part of my treatment. I've been on and off dialysis since the beginning of my senior year of high school — and almost lost my chance at college because of it. I've since gone through both peritoneal dialysis (PD) and hemodialysis — and it has not been easy. I've been dealing with recurring infections, I'm at risk of having to remove my catheter, and even with dialysis, my body has been really struggling without a functional kidney. It has been one of the hardest things I've ever faced.

Now I need a kidney transplant to move forward. I'm registered at the Inova Transplant Center under my legal name Thy Nguyen — please use that name when you contact them so they can connect you to my case.

If you think you might be able to help, reach out to Inova and let them know you're interested in donating.

Thank you for taking the time to read my story. It truly means everything. 🌸

The Process

What does living donation look like?

Living kidney donation is safer than most people think. Donors live normal, healthy lives with one kidney. The process is carefully managed and completely free for donors through Inova's program.

1

Reach Out

Contact Inova's living donor team directly. You'll have a confidential conversation with no obligation.

2

Free Evaluation

Complete a full medical and psychological evaluation at no cost to you. All medical expenses are covered.

3

Compatibility Check

Blood type and tissue matching are tested. If you're not a direct match for Thy, a kidney pair exchange may still make it possible.

4

Surgery & Recovery

The surgery is minimally invasive (laparoscopic). Most donors return to normal activities within 4–6 weeks.

Kidney Paired Donation

Not a blood type O donor? You can still save Selena.

Selena is blood type O — which means only blood type O donors are a direct match. But if you have a different blood type and still want to help, the Kidney Paired Donation (KPD) program was designed exactly for you. To get started, simply contact the Inova Living Donor Coordinator and ask about paired donation.

What Is Kidney Paired Donation?

Kidney Paired Donation (KPD) — also called kidney paired exchange — is a program that allows incompatible donor-recipient pairs to swap donors with other incompatible pairs so that everyone gets a compatible kidney.

Think of it like a matchmaking service. You want to give Selena a kidney, but your blood type doesn't match. Somewhere else in the country, there's another donor who is compatible with Selena — but they want to donate to someone else who your blood type does match. The program swaps the donations, and both recipients get the kidney they need.

Nobody goes without. Nobody "loses." It's one of the most powerful tools in transplant medicine today.

How a Paired Exchange Works Step by Step

🙋
Step 1

You Step Forward

You contact the Inova Living Donor Coordinator and express your wish to donate to Selena (Thy Nguyen). They evaluate you medically.

🔬
Step 2

Testing Finds No Match

Blood type or tissue testing shows you aren't a direct match for Selena. Instead of stopping there, the team enters you into the paired exchange pool.

🔄
Step 3

A Match Is Found

The national registry finds another pair where the other donor is compatible with Selena, and you are compatible with their recipient. A swap is arranged.

💗
Step 4

Everyone Gets a Kidney

You donate to their recipient. Their donor gives to Selena. Surgeries are coordinated simultaneously. Two lives — or more — are saved at once.

🌱 What About Altruistic (Non-Directed) Donors?

You don't even have to know Selena — or anyone — to change her life. An altruistic donor is someone who donates their kidney to a stranger with no specific recipient in mind. When entered into a KPD chain, an altruistic donor can set off a domino effect, triggering a chain of swaps that saves multiple people at once — including potentially Selena.

If you simply want to do something extraordinary for the world and are willing to donate, contact the Inova Living Donor Coordinator — and mention you'd be open to helping Selena (Thy Nguyen) if it's a match.

Common Questions

Things people want to know.

Will this affect my health long-term?

Studies show that people with one healthy kidney live just as long and as well as those with two. Donors are carefully screened to ensure it's safe before any decision is made.

Do I have to pay for anything?

No. All medical costs related to donation — including evaluation, surgery, and follow-up care — are covered. Some programs even help with travel and lost wages.

What blood types are compatible with Thy?

Please contact Inova directly to learn about Thy's specific compatibility needs. Even if you're not a direct match, the paired exchange program may still allow you to help.

How long is recovery?

Most donors go home within 2–3 days and return to desk work in 2–3 weeks. Full recovery, including physical labor, typically takes 4–6 weeks.

Can I back out at any time?

Yes. Donation is 100% voluntary. You can withdraw at any point in the process without any penalty or judgment, and your medical privacy is always protected.
